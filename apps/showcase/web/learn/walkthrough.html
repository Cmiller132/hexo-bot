End to end — one position, through the whole pipeline

This page replays one real position from the run’s own self-play history — the ply-40 decision of a recorded game: 40 stones down, player 0 placing the second stone of its turn, in a game that ran 119 decisions — through every stage of the pipeline, over the same 402-node support. The board in fig 1 never changes; only what each stage sees in it does. Step through the eight stages and watch the tensor shapes in the caption strip: every count on this page comes from running this position through the production featurizer.

The numbers are real, the weights are not shown: this site ships no trained checkpoint, so panels that would need learned values (the stage-05 fiber, the stage-08 candidate set) use fixed illustrative stand-ins and say so. The position itself — records, support layers, features, ray lengths, and every shape — is exported verbatim from the production featurizer to data/eq_walkthrough.json.

01facts

show stage 01 in fig 1 ↑

The position is 40 records of (q, r, owner, placement_index) plus the phase and this turn’s first stone. That is all that is ever stored or transmitted — training rows keep the placement history, the serve wire rebuilds everything from it, and feature planes are never written to disk. Here the phase is SecondStone: player 0’s first stone of the turn just landed at (8, −8), and the decision being traced is where its second stone goes.

02support

show stage 02 in fig 1 ↑

One BFS outward from the 40 stones produces the support set: every empty cell within hex-distance 4 of a stone (288 legal cells), the stones themselves, and the one-cell halo shell (74 cells) that gives edge convolutions neighbours to read — N = 402 nodes in [legal | stones | halo] order. Two aligned arrays carry the geometry: coords (402, 2) axial coordinates and nbr (402, 6) neighbour row-indices (missing neighbour → a sentinel that reads as a zero row). The legal-first ordering is a load-bearing choice: it is what stage 07 slices.

03features

show stage 03 in fig 1 ↑

The featurizer scans all 6 length-6 windows through every cell on every win axis — 402 × 3 × 6 = 7,236 windows — and walks 402 × 12 = 4,824 rays, producing feats (402, 46) (about a third of the entries are non-zero in this position) and raylen (402, 12). The 46 planes are 11 base scalars, 10 graded window quantities × 3 axes, 2 fork counts, and 3 global scalars; every “own” plane is relative to the side to move — player 0 here, so own tints blue in fig 1. Ray lengths never enter the planes: they feed the trunk convolutions’ visibility masks in stage 06.

04batching

show stage 04 in fig 1 ↑

Positions reach the GPU in padding groups sharing one node count, quantized to multiples of 64: this position’s group pads to Npad = 448, giving batch tensors (B, 448, 46), (B, 448, 6), (B, 448, 2), (B, 448, 12) and a (B, 448) boolean mask — 402 live rows, 46 dead ones. Fixed shapes buy few, replayable GPU launches; the waste is capped and the mask keeps it out of the math. How groups are packed is the serving page.

05stem

show stage 05 in fig 1 ↑

The stem — a tied 7-tap conv, group-norm, ReLU — lifts (402, 46) to (402, 192). From here on, each cell’s 192 channels are structured as a 12-slot × 16-channel fiber: one slot per lattice symmetry, 16 freely-learned orbit channels per slot. Rotate the board and the slots permute inside every cell while the cells move — the regular representation that makes every later weight constraint a pure index-gather. The full story is the symmetry page.

06trunk

show stage 06 in fig 1 ↑

The stream passes C C A C C A C A. Each C block runs two ray-tap convolutions — 7 gathers per cell: the center plus a blocker-aware, distance-weighted aggregate of up to 5 cells down each of the 6 directions — then a register refresh lets the 6 summary tokens count over all 402 cells with sigmoid-gated unnormalized sums. Each A block self-attends the joint sequence [6 tokens ; 402 cells] = 408 rows under a (3, 408, 408) position bias gathered from 237 learned rows. Through all eight blocks the shapes never change: cells (402, 192), tokens (6, 192). Mechanism details are on the network page.

07heads

show stage 07 in fig 1 ↑

After the final norm, the policy head emits one logit per node: (402,), of which the first 288 are the legal moves — the payoff of stage 02’s ordering. Softmaxed over that prefix, they are the search prior. The value head reads all 6 tokens, the masked mean of the cells, and the pre-final-norm token mean (so raw register counts survive the norm) — a 512-wide read → 65 bins on [−1, 1] → a scalar by softmax expectation.

08search

show stage 08 in fig 1 ↑

The prior and value enter the tree at this leaf. At the root the prior seeds the 16 Gumbel candidates; values back up the path that led here. After 256 visits the search emits the improved policy π′ as the training target and samples the move to play. That loop — Gumbel-Top-m, Sequential Halving, completed-Q — is the search page; what happens to π′ next is the training page.
