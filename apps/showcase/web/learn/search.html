The search — Gumbel AlphaZero

The search is Gumbel AlphaZero (Danihelka et al., 2022), implemented in Rust; Python is only called to evaluate leaf batches. Self-play searches 256 visits per decision. The design goal is different from classic PUCT: with a small visit budget, the search should produce a provably better policy — not just pick a move — and that improved policy π′ is what the network trains on.

01a tree of stones, not turns

A Hexo turn places two stones, but a tree edge is a single stone, not a turn. Each node stores its side-to-move; value backup flips sign by comparing the node’s player to the leaf’s player, so the two consecutive same-player plies of a turn back up correctly without any turn-level special-casing — the sign follows the player comparison, not depth parity. The engine checks for six-in-a-row after every stone, so a win on the first stone of a turn is a terminal node whose second stone never exists. Terminal nodes back up exact ±1, not a network value.

02the root: gumbel-top-m + sequential halving

One Gumbel noise value g(a) is drawn per legal root action, and the top m = 16 actions by g(a) + logits(a) become the candidates. The tactical-safety scan (§04 below) force-includes any threat-participating cells that missed the cut, so the candidate set can exceed 16. Sequential Halving then spends the 256-visit budget: candidates get equal visits per round, and the worse half is eliminated each round — 4 rounds at m = 16 — ranked by

g(a) + logits(a) + σ(completedQ(a)), σ(q) = (50 + maxb N(b)) · q

completedQ is the child’s mean value where visited, and a visit-weighted blend of the node value and the visited children’s prior-weighted average where not. The σ transform is the heart of the scheme: as visits accumulate, the scale 50 + maxb N(b) grows, and measured value counts for more and more against the fixed prior-plus-noise term the candidates started from.

The illustrative values are rigged the way real searches often are: the highest-logit candidate is not the best move. Watch the ranking re-sort as σ(q̂) outgrows the prior — and note the end state: the finalists’ visit counts are equal by construction (the schedule gave them identical quotas), while π′ concentrates on the winner. Under Sequential Halving, visit counts are a schedule artifact; the recorded training target is π′.

03the interior: visit shares chase π′

Below the root, selection is deterministic — no noise is injected anywhere in the search. Each interior node computes its own improved policy π′ = softmax(logits + σ(completedQ)) over its candidates and visits the action maximizing

π′(a) − N(a) / (1 + Σb N(b))

— the action whose visit share lags its improved-policy share the most. Applied repeatedly, the rule is a derandomized sampler: the visit distribution tracks π′ as closely as integer counts allow, with no explorative randomness needed. Leaves are selected up to 96 at a time per game under a virtual-loss of 1.0 (each pending leaf counts as a visit until its evaluation returns), evaluated as one batch, then backed up.

Nothing here is stochastic: no Dirichlet noise, no forced playouts, anywhere. Exploration comes from exactly three places — the root Gumbel draw, the play-time temperature (§04), and the openings mix (§05).

04choosing the move — and what gets recorded

Two different outputs leave the root, and they are not the same thing. The recorded training target is π′ at the root — the improved policy — not the visit histogram; the raw root logits are recorded alongside it. The played move is sampled from the root visit distribution under a temperature schedule T(ply) = max(0.15, 0.5ply/20), with candidates eliminated in the first halving round removed from the sampling distribution.

05the continuous scheduler

256 games run concurrently against one GPU. Games drop pending leaves into a shared queue; at ~1024 pending — or when nothing can progress — the batch flushes to the evaluator. Duplicate positions are evaluated once per batch and cached across the run (a 262,144-state cache). Finished games are replaced immediately, so the queue never drains behind a stragglers’ tail. Games cap at 256 plies; there is no resignation.

Openings supply the last exploration source: 35% of games begin with a few plies — up to 8 — sampled directly from the raw prior at temperature 1.4, without search; those plies produce no training rows.

That evaluator boundary is where the search hands off: how one GPU turns ~1024-leaf flushes from 256 games into fixed-shape forward passes is the serving.
