\section{Zone-carrying certificates}
\label{sec:certificates}

At an AND node, the solver may face hundreds of legal defender replies.  A
zone-carrying certificate gives each omitted reply a checkable dismissal
argument tied to the remaining certificate.

\subsection{Transfer lemmas}

Every comparison of two positions must account for the same three ways in
which a stone can affect the game.

\begin{lemma}[Difference accounting]
\label{lem:L3}
Let $P'=P\mathbin{\pm}\{x\}$ differ from $P$ by one stone at the cell $x$.
Every game-relevant predicate difference between $P$ and $P'$ is confined to
the following channels:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(C\arabic*)}]\upshape
  \item the occupancy and ownership of $x$;
  \item the masks of the $18$ windows containing $x$, and hence completion,
    alive or dead status, threats, $\ownwinnow$, $\hit$, and $\mhs$; and
  \item the legality frontier
    $\{c:\dist(c,x)\le8\}\setminus\operatorname{dom}\sigma$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The transition rule depends on occupancy at the placed cell, legality, and
the masks used for termination.  The tactical predicates are functions of
window masks, which change only for windows containing $x$.  Legality
justifications change through $x$ only within distance $8$.  There are no
other state components.
\end{proof}

Permanence lets later witnesses be interpreted at earlier points of the same
play.

\begin{lemma}[Permanence]
\label{lem:L4}
Window masks are monotone nondecreasing along every play.  Dead windows remain
dead.  A window alive for $X$ at ply $t$ is alive for $X$ at every earlier
ply.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Stones are never removed by \cref{def:D3,def:D4}.
\end{proof}

Adding inert defender stones transfers the cells, masks, legality, and
completion events used later in the proof.

\begin{lemma}[Inert-extension transfer]
\label{lem:L5}
Let $P'=P+X$, where $X$ is a finite set of defender stones.  Let
$\mathcal W$ be a set of windows and $C$ a set of cells such that no member of
$X$ lies in a member of $\mathcal W$ and $X\cap C=\varnothing$.  Then:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]\upshape
  \item every cell of $C$ empty in $P$ is empty in $P'$;
  \item every $W\in\mathcal W$ has identical masks in $P$ and $P'$;
  \item $\Legal(P)\setminus X\subseteq\Legal(P')$, so every cell of $C$
    legal in $P$ is legal in $P'$; and
  \item the $\A$-complete windows of $P'$ are exactly the $\A$-complete
    windows of $P$ disjoint from $X$.
\end{enumerate}
Thus an $\A$-completion on an $X$-free window transfers from $P$ to $P'$, and
every $\A$-completion in $P'$ was available in $P$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Parts (a) and (b) follow from occupancy and incident-window masks.  Adding
stones can only add legality justifications, while occupancy removes the
cells of $X$.  An $\A$-complete window contains no defender stone, so windows
meeting $X$ are blocked in $P'$ and the other masks agree.
\end{proof}

Deleting defender stones is monotone for attacker tactics, but it requires a
separate legality condition for attacker placements.

\begin{lemma}[Deletion monotonicity and well-formed legality]
\label{lem:L6}
Let $P''=P-Y$, where $Y$ is a finite set of defender stones of $P$.  Then:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]\upshape
  \item every $\A$-threat of $P$ is an $\A$-threat of $P''$, so
    $\mhs(P'')\ge\mhs(P)$ for the defender and defender
    $\lambda^1$-\textsc{loss} verdicts transfer from $P$ to $P''$;
  \item every $\A$-complete window of $P$ is $\A$-complete in $P''$;
  \item a cell empty in $P$ is empty or newly empty in $P''$; and
  \item legality may shrink.  Every relied-upon attacker placement must be
    within distance $8$ of a stone outside $Y$.  In the uses below it is
    within distance $3$ of an attacker stone, as supplied for threat creation
    by \cref{thm:T2}, or within distance $8$ of an unaffected root stone.
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Deleting defender stones can only revive attacker windows and cannot remove
an attacker completion.  Emptiness follows from \cref{def:D3}.  The last
condition restores the legality-frontier channel after deletion.
\end{proof}

Completion counting isolates the defender placements relevant to one
window.

\begin{lemma}[Completion counting]
\label{lem:L7}
If $\cnt_\D(W,P)=s$ and the defender makes at most $D$ placements at plies
strictly before $T$, then $W$ cannot become $\D$-complete before $T$ unless
$s+D\ge6$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
A completion needs six defender stones in $W$.  At most $s$ are present in
$P$, and at most $D$ more can be placed before $T$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Certificate grammar and local clocks}

A certificate records the searched tree and checkable witness data for every
way in which a selected path can resolve.

\begin{definition}[Certificate grammar]
\label{def:D9}
A \emph{certificate} $\mathcal C$ for ``$\A$ wins from $P_0$'' is a finite
rooted tree whose nodes have exact positions and mover/budget labels.  The
root is $P_0$ and is nonterminal.  The verifier recomputes each path-derived
ply clock; leaf-ply is the index of the last placement on the path to a leaf.
Every edge is the exact legal successor under \cref{def:D4}, and no edge is
defender-terminal.  Every maximal node has one of these types.
\begin{itemize}
  \item An \emph{OR-COMPLETION leaf}, with $\A$ to place, names one
    designated placement, its $\A$-complete witness window, and its completion
    ply.
  \item A \emph{WIN leaf}, with $\A$ to place, is a verifier-checked
    $\ownwinnow$ position.  It names $\A$-alive count-$5$ witness windows for
    either budget, or count-$4$ witnesses when the budget is $2$, and declares
    resolution within the current attacker turn.
  \item A \emph{LOSS leaf}, with $\D$ to place and budget $b$, names an
    $\A$-threat family $\mathcal T$ whose empty-set family has transversal
    number $\tau(\mathcal T)>b$, and carries a verifier-checked
    $\neg\ownwinnow$ fact.  For every complete nonterminal remainder $H$ of
    the defender turn, consisting of exactly $b$ placements unless the game
    ends earlier, some $W_H\in\mathcal T$ satisfies
    $E(W_H,P_L)\cap H=\varnothing$.  The attacker then fills its at most two
    empties.  The declared worst-case resolution is leaf-ply${}+b+2$.
    The verifier may require $|\mathcal T|\le3$ for $b=1$ and
    $|\mathcal T|\le6$ for $b=2$, by \cref{lem:L13}.
\end{itemize}
An internal OR node has one designated attacker placement and its exact
child.  An internal AND node $N$ has a nonempty searched set $S(N)$ of legal
replies, one exact child per searched reply, and a dismissal claim for every
other legal reply.  The verifier retains the internal defender
$\neg\ownwinnow$ check as a diagnostic.  It is derivable under the completion
zone by \cref{lem:L14}, but remains mandatory at LOSS leaves and an explicit
premise of \cref{thm:T6}.  Every resolution is finite, and the global horizon
$T$ is the maximum declared resolution over all root-to-leaf paths.
Finite consistently labelled DAGs are admitted by \cref{def:D18,thm:T10}.
\end{definition}

The remaining defender work below a node is measured locally rather than by
the maximum resolution elsewhere in the tree.

\begin{definition}[Admissible local defender budget]
\label{def:D14}
The exact local budget $B(N)$, in defender placements, is given by
\[
\begin{aligned}
B(\text{OR-COMPLETION})&=B(\text{WIN})=0,&
B(\text{LOSS},b)&=b,\\
B(\text{OR})&=B(\text{child}),&
B(\text{AND})&=1+\max_C B(C).
\end{aligned}
\]
The two placements of a defender turn are two edges, and a LOSS leaf adds its
remaining $b$ placements.  A verifier may instead accept any nonnegative
integral labels satisfying $B(L)\ge b$ at LOSS leaves,
$B(N)\ge B(C)$ on OR edges, and $B(N)\ge1+B(C)$ on AND edges.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
The first version of this paper required the global horizon count
$\DefCount(P_N,T)$; that requirement was since shown unnecessary.
\end{remark}

Only future attacker placements and leaf-entry witness empties must remain
unoccupied by dismissed defender stones.

\begin{definition}[Compressed obligations]
\label{def:D10}
Write $E(W,P)=W\setminus\Stones(P)$.  A \emph{live obligation role} below
$N$ is one of two types:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\arabic*)}]\upshape
  \item a future designated certificate attacker placement, including an
    OR-COMPLETION placement; or
  \item a triple $(L,W,y)$ for a named WIN or LOSS witness $W$ at a leaf $L$
    and a cell $y\in E(W,P_L)$.
\end{enumerate}
Witness-empty roles represent every possible leaf continuation placement,
including the adaptive LOSS choice.  If $\Omega(N)$ is the set of all such
roles at reachable descendants, define
\[
  \operatorname{Obl}(\mathcal C,N)=\Prot(N)
  :=\left\{y:\begin{array}{c}\text{some live role in }\Omega(N)\\[-1mm]
    \text{is carried by }y\end{array}\right\}.
\]
An attacker-placement role is live through its check immediately before the
OR step.  A witness-empty role is live through its check at leaf entry.  Each
is discharged immediately afterward.  Reachable-descendant nesting gives
$\Prot(M)\subseteq\Prot(N)$ whenever $M$ descends from $N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
The first version of this paper protected every cell of each witness window;
that requirement was since shown unnecessary.
\end{remark}

Different cells can be needed at different depths, so each role has its own
defender-placement deadline.

\begin{definition}[Cell-specific deadlines]
\label{def:D15}
The deadline of a role $\rho$ is its designated attacker placement or, for a
WIN or LOSS witness-empty role, leaf entry.  At a node $N$ from which $\rho$
is reachable, $r_N(\rho)$ is an integral upper bound on defender placements
before that deadline.  Exact ranks count the maximum number of AND edges on a
reachable path.  The verifier checks $r=0$ at the deadline,
$r_N(\rho)\ge r_C(\rho)$ across an OR edge, and
$r_N(\rho)\ge1+r_C(\rho)$ across an AND edge while the role is live.  For a
cell carrying several roles, put
\[
  r_N(y)=\max\{r_N(\rho):\rho\in\Omega(N)\text{ is carried by }y\}.
\]
A role is discharged only after its $r=0$ deadline check.  A seed band is
formed only at an internal AND node with $r_N(y)\ge1$.  LOSS witness empties
are protected only through leaf entry; their adaptive contract controls the
remaining $b$ placements.  Defender-completion windows use
\cref{def:D14,def:D16}, not role ranks.  The uniform admissible choice is
$r_N(\rho)=B(N)$ before the deadline.
\end{definition}

Completion risk also has a separate local clock for every window.

\begin{definition}[Per-window defender exposure]
\label{def:D16}
For each window $W$, let $E_Q^\D(W)$ be the maximum number of future defender
placements before the certificate attacker wins or first places in $W$.  Its
exact recurrence is
\[
E_Q^\D(W)=
\begin{cases}
0,&Q\text{ is WIN or OR-COMPLETION},\\
b,&Q\text{ is LOSS with defender budget }b,\\
0,&Q\text{ is an OR whose move enters }W,\\
E_C^\D(W),&Q\text{ is any other ordinary OR},\\
1+\max_C E_C^\D(W),&Q\text{ is an internal AND}.
\end{cases}
\]
If $W$ is already not alive for $\D$, set $E_Q^\D(W)=0$.  An attacker move
\emph{enters} $W$ when $W$ becomes permanently not alive for $\D$; it need
not become dead.  Overlapping windows have separate clocks.  The stopping
attacker placement is also a live obligation, so the obligation zone keeps
the stop playable in the real game.
\end{definition}

The local labels decrease along every selected path and preserve the relevant
stone-count bounds.

\begin{lemma}[Local-clock and nesting facts]
\label{lem:L11}
If a descendant $M$ is reached from $N$ after $k$ defender placements, then
\[
\begin{gathered}
B(M)+k\le B(N),\\
\cnt_\D(W,P_M)+B(M)\le\cnt_\D(W,P_N)+B(N).
\end{gathered}
\]
Every selected path below $N$, including a LOSS remainder, contains at most
$B(N)$ defender placements before its declared attacker resolution.  For
every still-live role $\rho$,
$r_M(\rho)+k\le r_N(\rho)$.  Before the attacker enters $W$,
\[
\begin{gathered}
E_M^\D(W)+k\le E_N^\D(W),\\
\cnt_\D(W,P_M)+E_M^\D(W)
 \le\cnt_\D(W,P_N)+E_N^\D(W).
\end{gathered}
\]
Also $E_N^\D(W)\le B(N)$, every exact rank
$r_N^*(\rho)\le B(N)$, and $\Prot(M)\subseteq\Prot(N)$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Sum the verifier inequalities along the path.  Each AND edge contributes one
and each OR edge contributes zero; the AND maximum covers the selected child,
and the LOSS bound covers its remainder.  A window gains at most one defender
stone per counted defender edge.  The exposure recurrence gives the analogous
inequalities until its stop and is bounded pointwise by the budget recurrence.
An exact role deadline occurs no later than the path resolution.  The last
claim is reachable-descendant inclusion.
\end{proof}

The zone separates obligation occupancy from the two forms of completion
risk.  Touched windows need no frontier term; virgin windows need a seed band.

\begin{definition}[Zone-carrying certificate]
\label{def:D11}
For $d(c,W)=\min_{w\in W}d(c,w)$ and
$B_r(U)=\{c:\exists u\in U,\ d(c,u)\le r\}$, define at each internal AND
node $N$
\[
\begin{aligned}
Z_{\rm dir}(N)&=\Prot(N)\cap\Legal(P_N),\\
Z_{\rm seed}(N)&=\bigcup_{\substack{y\in\Prot(N)\setminus
  (\Legal(P_N)\cup\Stones(P_N))\\ r_N(y)\ge1}}
  \left(\Legal(P_N)\cap B_{8(r_N(y)-1)}(\{y\})\right),\\
Z_{\rm touch}(N)&=\bigcup\left\{E(W,P_N):
  \begin{array}{l}W\text{ is alive for }\D,\ \cnt_\D(W,P_N)\ge1,\\[-1mm]
  \cnt_\D(W,P_N)+E_N^\D(W)\ge6\end{array}\right\},\\
Z_{\rm virgin}(N)&=\left\{c\in\Legal(P_N):
  \begin{array}{l}\text{some all-empty window }W\text{ has }E_N^\D(W)\ge6,\\[-1mm]
  d(c,W)\le8(E_N^\D(W)-6)
  \end{array}\right\}.
\end{aligned}
\]
Every cell of $Z_{\rm touch}$ is legal by \cref{lem:L1}.  The certificate is
\emph{zone-carrying} when $S(N)$ is independently nonempty at every internal
AND node and the following clauses hold.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(Z\arabic*)}]\upshape
  \setcounter{enumi}{1}
  \item \label{zone:Z2}
    $S(N)\supseteq Z_{\rm dir}(N)\cup Z_{\rm touch}(N)\cup
    Z_{\rm virgin}(N)$.
  \setcounter{enumi}{3}
  \item \label{zone:Z4}
    Every certificate attacker placement, including a placement allowed by a
    leaf contract, lies within distance $8$ of an attacker stone in its
    predecessor position or of a root stone unaffected by the coupling.
\end{enumerate}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(Z5$'$)}]\upshape
  \setcounter{enumi}{4}
  \item \label{zone:Z5prime}
    $S(N)\supseteq Z_{\rm seed}(N)$.
\end{enumerate}
With the uniform choice $r_N(y)=B(N)$, clause \textup{(Z5$'$)} becomes
\[
S(N)\supseteq\Legal(P_N)\cap B_{8(B(N)-1)}
  \bigl(\Prot(N)\setminus(\Legal(P_N)\cup\Stones(P_N))\bigr).
\]
All four components are finite and verifier-enumerable.  The virgin query is
inverted from each legal candidate over the finite radius allowed by
$E_N^\D(W)\le B(N)$.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
The first version of this paper required current threat-hitting cells as
clause \textup{(Z1)}; that requirement was since shown unnecessary.
\end{remark}

The ranked seed guard prevents the first real-only occupation of a protected
cell, which is the property used by the coupling.

\begin{lemma}[First protected occupation]
\label{lem:L9prime}
Suppose direct legal protected cells are searched and every ghost-legal
dismissal $x$ at $N$ lies outside radius $8(B(N)-1)$ of
\[
  \Prot(N)\setminus(\Legal(P_N)\cup\Stones(P_N)).
\]
Then no defender placement creates a real-only stone in the current protected
set.  In the ranked form, replace $B(N)$ for each target $y$ by its maximum
live role rank $r_N(y)$; the conclusion holds through that role's deadline.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Assume that the first violation is a defender placement at $y$.  If $y$ is
ghost-legal, then $y\in Z_{\rm dir}$ and is searched, so it is placed in both
games.  Otherwise trace its real-only legality witness backward through
real-only defender stones to the first ghost-legal dismissed seed $x_0$.  If
$y$ is the $p$th defender placement in this chain, counting $x_0$, there are
at most $p-1$ radius-$8$ links and
\[
  d(x_0,y)\le8(p-1).
\]
Protection nesting puts $y\in\Prot(N_{x_0})$.  At the violation it is still
ghost-empty and ghost-illegal.  Ghost legality of an unoccupied cell is
monotone, and permanence makes it a non-stone at $N_{x_0}$.  The local budget
gives $p\le B(N_{x_0})$; in the ranked form, the still-live role gives
$p\le r_{N_{x_0}}(y)$.  Thus $x_0$ lay in the applicable seed band, contrary
to its dismissal.
\end{proof}

The uniform radius is sharp: a ghost-legal seed can be followed by $B-1$
successive distance-$8$ placements, with the protected target last.

\begin{remark}
The first version of this paper required an $8\DefCount(P_N,T)$ frontier band;
that requirement was since shown unnecessary.
\end{remark}

The proof compares the actual play with a certificate walk using canonical
defender-stone differences.

\begin{definition}[Real--ghost coupling]
\label{def:D12}
The real position $R$ and ghost position $G$ have equal ply, mover, and
budget, and identical attacker stones.  Define
\[
X=\Stones_\D(R)\setminus\Stones_\D(G),\qquad
Y=\Stones_\D(G)\setminus\Stones_\D(R).
\]
The maintained invariants are: every $x\in X$ entered at a dismissal whose
ordinary zone test or selected \cref{def:D17} envelope certified it outside
the live obligations; every $y\in Y$ entered as a searched ghost filler; and
$X\cap\Prot(N)=\varnothing$ at each visited node.  The previous lemma supplies
the last invariant for ghost-illegal dismissals, and obligation nesting
preserves it after descent.  A history $\widehat X\supseteq X$ contains every
cell ever added to $X$ and never deletes cells.  For every window $W$,
\begin{equation}
\cnt_\D(W,R)=\cnt_\D(W,G)+|X\cap W|-|Y\cap W|
 \le\cnt_\D(W,G)+|\widehat X\cap W|.
\tag{MI}\label{eq:MI}
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

The touched--virgin split excludes every real defender completion before the
mapped attacker stop, including mixed legality chains.

\begin{lemma}[Split completion safety]
\label{lem:L12}
In the coupling of \cref{def:D12}, under \cref{zone:Z2,zone:Z4} and
\hyperref[zone:Z5prime]{clause \textup{(Z5$'$)}}, no real defender play
completes a fixed window $W$ before the mapped certificate attacker wins or
first enters $W$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Suppose $W$ is first completed in the real game before that stop.  Shared
attacker stones and permanence make it alive for $\D$ throughout the relevant
ghost prefix.  If no $W$-cell was ever dismissed, then
$\widehat X\cap W=\varnothing$ and \eqref{eq:MI} forces the ghost line to have
at least the real defender count.  This contradicts the exact successors and
the ban on defender-terminal edges.

Otherwise let $x\in W$ be the first ever-dismissed real-only fill, at $N$.
If the ghost already has a defender stone in $W$, every empty of $W$ is
ghost-legal.  Before $x$, \eqref{eq:MI} bounds the real count by the ghost
count.  The real placements from $x$ through completion are covered by
$E_N^\D(W)$, so
\[
  \cnt_\D(W,P_N)+E_N^\D(W)\ge6.
\]
The cell $x$ lies in $Z_{\rm touch}$ and should have been searched.

Suppose instead that the ghost window is virgin.  If $x$ is ghost-legal, all
six fills remain in the exposure count.  Hence $E_N^\D(W)\ge6$ and
$d(x,W)=0$, so $x\in Z_{\rm virgin}$.  If $x$ is ghost-illegal, trace its
real-only legality chain to the first ghost-legal dismissed seed $x_0$.  With
$j$ radius-$8$ links before the chain reaches $W$,
\[
  d(x_0,W)\le8j,\qquad E_{N_{x_0}}^\D(W)\ge j+6.
\]
The second inequality counts the seed and chain tempo and all six fills before
the exposure stop.  Thus $x_0\in Z_{\rm virgin}$.  A searched fill that
touches $W$ moves later nodes to the touched case.  Independent seeds,
interleaving, and overlapping windows are covered by the causal chain for the
first dismissed fill of each window.  The live obligation for the
attacker-in-$W$ move keeps the exposure stop valid in the real game.

At a LOSS remainder, the coupling stops at leaf entry.  If $W$ has an
ever-dismissed fill, the same count remains valid because the exposure
includes the leaf's $b$ placements.  If it has none, \eqref{eq:MI} at the leaf
bounds the real count by the ghost count.  The mandatory leaf
$\neg\ownwinnow$ check bounds the latter by $3$ at $b=2$ and $4$ at $b=1$,
so the remaining $b$ placements reach at most $5$.
\end{proof}

The virgin radius is sharp at exposure $7$: a legal seed at distance $8$ can
be followed by the first window fill and the five remaining fills.
