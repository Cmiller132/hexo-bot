\section{Zone-carrying certificates}
\label{sec:certificates}

At an AND node, the solver may face hundreds of legal defender replies.  We
want a searched set at each such node for which every omitted reply carries a
proof of dismissal.  No unbounded static set can provide this guarantee, so
the construction is parameterized by the certificate's own placement horizon.

\subsection{Transfer lemmas}

Every comparison of two positions must account for the same three ways in
which a stone can affect the game.

\begin{lemma}[Difference accounting]
\label{lem:L3}
Let $P'=P\mathbin{\pm}\{x\}$ differ from $P$ by one stone at the cell $x$.
Every game-relevant predicate difference between $P$ and $P'$ is confined to
the following channels:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(C\arabic*)}]\upshape
  \item the occupancy of $x$, including its availability as a move and its
    ownership;
  \item the masks of the $18$ windows containing $x$, and hence every
    window-mask-derived predicate: completion, alive or dead status, threat
    status, $\ownwinnow$, $\hit$, and $\mhs$;
  \item the legality frontier
    \[
      \{c:\dist(c,x)\le 8\}\setminus\operatorname{dom}\sigma,
    \]
    whose cells may gain or lose a legality justification through $x$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
By \cref{def:D4}, the transition relation depends on occupancy at the placed
cell, the legality predicate, and the window masks used for termination.  By
\cref{def:D5,def:D6}, every tactical predicate is a function of window masks.
Only windows containing $x$ change masks.  By \cref{def:D4}, only cells within
distance $8$ of $x$ can gain or lose a legality justification through $x$.
There are no other state components.
\end{proof}

Thus a transfer argument is complete exactly when it accounts for occupancy,
incident-window masks, and the legality frontier.

Permanence lets later witnesses be interpreted at earlier points of the same
play.

\begin{lemma}[Permanence]
\label{lem:L4}
Window masks are monotone nondecreasing along every play.  Dead windows remain
dead.  A window alive for $X$ at ply $t$ is alive for $X$ at every earlier
ply.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Stones are never removed by \cref{def:D3,def:D4}.
\end{proof}

Adding inert defender stones transfers the cells, masks, legality, and
completion events used later in the proof.

\begin{lemma}[Inert-extension transfer]
\label{lem:L5}
Let $P'=P+X$, where $X$ is a finite set of defender stones.  Let
$\mathcal W$ be a set of windows and $C$ a set of cells such that
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]\upshape
  \item no $x\in X$ lies in any $W\in\mathcal W$; and
  \item $X\cap C=\varnothing$.
\end{enumerate}
Then:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]\upshape
  \item every cell of $C$ empty in $P$ is empty in $P'$;
  \item every $W\in\mathcal W$ has identical masks in $P$ and $P'$;
  \item $\Legal(P)\setminus X\subseteq\Legal(P')$; in particular, every
    cell of $C$ legal in $P$ is legal in $P'$; and
  \item the $\A$-complete windows of $P'$ are exactly the $\A$-complete
    windows of $P$ that are disjoint from $X$.
\end{enumerate}
Consequently, an $\A$-completion on a window known to be disjoint from $X$
transfers from $P$ to $P'$, and every $\A$-completion in $P'$ was available
in $P$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Parts (a) and (b) follow from occupancy and incident-window masks in
\cref{lem:L3}.  For (c), adding stones can only add legality justifications,
while occupancy removes precisely the cells of $X$.  For (d), an
$\A$-complete window must contain no defender stone.  Windows meeting $X$ are
blocked in $P'$, and the masks of the other windows agree.
\end{proof}

Deleting defender stones is monotone for attacker tactics, but it requires a
separate legality condition for attacker placements.

\begin{lemma}[Deletion monotonicity and well-formed legality]
\label{lem:L6}
Let $P''=P-Y$, where $Y$ is a finite set of defender stones of $P$.  Then:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]\upshape
  \item every $\A$-threat window of $P$ is an $\A$-threat window of $P''$,
    so $\mhs(P'')\ge\mhs(P)$ for the defender and defender
    $\lambda^1$-\textsc{loss} verdicts transfer from $P$ to $P''$;
  \item every $\A$-complete window of $P$ is $\A$-complete in $P''$;
  \item a cell empty in $P$ is empty or newly empty in $P''$, never newly
    occupied; and
  \item legality may shrink.  A deletion argument must therefore satisfy
    \emph{well-formed legality}: every attacker placement on which it relies
    lies within distance $8$ of a stone not in $Y$.  In the applications
    below, it lies within distance $3$ of an attacker stone, as follows
    automatically for threat-creating moves from \cref{thm:T2}, or it lies
    within distance $8$ of a root stone.
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Deleting defender stones can only revive attacker windows, proving (a) and
(b).  Part (c) follows from \cref{def:D3}.  Part (d) follows from the legality
rule in \cref{def:D4}; the stated witness restores the legality-frontier
channel of \cref{lem:L3} for every relied-upon cell.
\end{proof}

Completion counting bounds which defender windows can matter before a fixed
horizon.

\begin{lemma}[Completion counting]
\label{lem:L7}
If $\cnt_\D(W,P)=s$ and the defender makes at most $D$ placements at plies
strictly before $T$, then no play makes $W$ $\D$-complete before ply $T$
unless $s+D\ge6$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
A $\D$-completion needs six defender stones in $W$.  At most $s$ are present
in $P$, and at most $D$ more can be placed before $T$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Certificate grammar}

A certificate records both the searched game tree and verifier-checkable
witness data at every way in which that tree can resolve.

\begin{definition}[Certificate grammar]
\label{def:D9}
A \emph{certificate} $\mathcal C$ for ``$\A$ wins from $P_0$ by horizon $T$,''
where $T$ is an absolute ply index, is a finite tree whose nodes carry a
position and mover/budget.  The verifier checks every clause of the following
grammar syntactically.
\begin{itemize}
  \item The root position is $P_0$ and is nonterminal.  Every node has a
    path-derived clock equal to the root ply plus its depth.  The verifier
    recomputes this clock.  Every resolution label refers to it, and
    \emph{leaf-ply} is the index of the last placement completed on the path
    to a leaf.
  \item Every child is the exact successor, under \cref{def:D4}, of the
    placement on its edge.  Every placement is legal at its parent.  No edge
    is defender-terminal; a searched defender reply that completes a defender
    window makes the certificate invalid.
  \item Every maximal node is typed.  An OR node whose designated placement
    completes is itself an \emph{OR-COMPLETION leaf}.  Every internal AND
    node has a nonempty searched set.  At every AND node, and again at every
    LOSS leaf, the verifier checks that $\ownwinnow$ is false for the
    defender.
  \item An \emph{OR node}, at which $\A$ places, has one designated
    placement.  If it completes a window, the node names the witness window
    $W^*$ and the completion ply.  Otherwise it has the successor as its one
    child.
  \item An \emph{AND node} $N$, at which $\D$ places with budget $b$, carries
    a searched set $S(N)$ of legal replies, one child for each searched reply,
    and a dismissal claim for every other legal reply.
  \item A \emph{WIN leaf}, at which $\A$ is to place, is a verifier-checked
    $\ownwinnow$ position.  It names an $\A$-alive witness window with count
    $5$ for either budget, or count $4$ when $b=2$.  Completion therefore
    occurs within the attacker's current turn with no intervening defender
    placement.  The leaf names its witness window or windows and a resolution
    ply at most $T$.
  \item A \emph{LOSS leaf}, at which $\D$ is to place with budget $b$ and has
    a $\lambda^1$-\textsc{loss}, names a witness family $\mathcal T$ of
    $\A$-threat windows whose empty sets have hitting number greater than
    $b$.  The verifier re-derives this fact and rechecks that
    $\ownwinnow$ is false.  Its continuation contract is adaptive: for every
    complete, nonterminal remainder $H$ of the defender's current turn,
    consisting of exactly $b$ placements unless the game ends earlier, some
    $W_H\in\mathcal T$ survives because its empty-cell set at the leaf is
    disjoint from $H$.  The
    attacker then completes the at most two empty cells of $W_H$ during the
    following turn.  The defender places at plies
    $\text{leaf-ply}+1,\ldots,\text{leaf-ply}+b$, and the attacker places at
    $\text{leaf-ply}+b+1$ and, for a count-$4$ witness,
    $\text{leaf-ply}+b+2$.  The declared worst-case resolution ply is
    $\text{leaf-ply}+b+2\le T$.  The leaf names no fixed placement list;
    the contract quantifies over $H$.
\end{itemize}
The horizon $T$ is the maximum resolution ply over the tree.  Every
$\lambda^1$ fact at a leaf is tied to named windows and a checkable
continuation contract.
\end{definition}

The core collects every cell whose masks or occupancy are used by a future
certificate witness.

\begin{definition}[Certificate core]
\label{def:D10}
For a node $N$, define $\operatorname{core}(\mathcal C,N)$ as the union over
the subtree rooted at $N$ of
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]\upshape
  \item every cell of every named witness window, including OR completions,
    WIN leaves, and LOSS-leaf families; and
  \item every attacker placement cell, including leaf-continuation
    placements.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

The protected set adds every defender window that can still complete within
the certificate horizon.  Its four conditions cover tactical consistency,
witness preservation, frontier growth, and attacker legality.

\begin{definition}[Zone-carrying certificate]
\label{def:D11}
At an AND node $N$ with position $P_N$, put
\[
  D_N:=\DefCount(P_N,T)
\]
and define
\[
\begin{split}
  \Prot(N)={}&\operatorname{core}(\mathcal C,N)\\
  &{}\cup\!\bigcup\left\{
    E(W,P_N):
    \begin{array}{l}
      W\text{ is alive for }\D\text{ at }P_N,\\[-1mm]
      \cnt_\D(W,P_N)+D_N\ge6
    \end{array}
  \right\},
\end{split}
\]
where $E(W,P)$ is the set of empty cells of $W$ in $P$.  For a cell set
$U$, let
\[
  B(r;U)=\{c:\exists y\in U,\ \dist(c,y)\le r\}.
\]
The certificate is \emph{zone-carrying} if every AND node $N$ satisfies:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(Z\arabic*)}]\upshape
  \item \label{zone:Z1}
    $S(N)$ contains the legal empty cells of every $\A$-threat window of
    $P_N$.  This condition is retained as a redundant safeguard for interior
    $\lambda^1$ consistency: a current threat need not occur in any later
    witness family, and hitting cells are already the solver's first
    candidates.
  \item \label{zone:Z2}
    $S(N)\supseteq\Prot(N)\cap\Legal(P_N)$.  Thus every protected legal cell
    is searched.
  \setcounter{enumi}{4}
  \item \label{zone:Z5}
    \[
      S(N)\supseteq \Legal(P_N)\cap
      B\!\left(8D_N;
        \Prot(N)\setminus(\Legal(P_N)\cup\Stones(P_N))
      \right).
    \]
    A defender has at most $D_N$ placements before the horizon, and each can
    extend legality by at most $8$.  The horizon-scaled band closes the
    multi-hop corridor in which successive dismissed stones approach
    protected territory while later links are ghost-illegal.  The band is
    empty when $\Prot(N)\subseteq\Legal(P_N)\cup\Stones(P_N)$.
  \setcounter{enumi}{3}
  \item \label{zone:Z4}
    Every attacker placement in the subtree of $N$ is within distance $8$ of
    an attacker stone of its predecessor position or of a stone of $P_0$.
    This condition preserves the legality of attacker placements when
    defender stones are deleted.  It is automatic for threat-creating
    generators by \cref{thm:T2}.
\end{enumerate}

When $D_N\le5$, both terms of $\Prot(N)$ are finite and verifier-enumerable:
a completion-guard window has at least one defender stone and is therefore
touched.  When $D_N\ge6$, every all-empty window qualifies.  Hence
$\Prot(N)\cap\Legal(P_N)$ contains every legal cell in any window alive for
$\D$, and the frontier condition also covers its band.  The only remaining
dismissible cells are deep-interior cells lying in no $\D$-alive window;
every window through such a cell is attacker-touched or dead, so the cell can
never contribute to a defender completion by \cref{lem:L4,lem:L7}.  This is
  the exact boundary of the large-budget case $D_N\ge6$, not a claim that pruning always
vanishes.
\end{definition}

Protected cells can disappear under descent, but new protected cells cannot
appear.

\begin{proposition}[Monotonicity of protection]
\label{prop:prot-mono}
If $M$ is a descendant AND node of $N$, then
$\Prot(M)\subseteq\Prot(N)$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The core is a subtree union and is monotone under descent.  Along the play
from $N$ to $M$, $\cnt_\D(W,\cdot)$ increases by the number of intervening
defender placements in $W$, while $\DefCount(\cdot,T)$ decreases by the
number of all intervening defender placements, which is at least as large.
Their sum is nonincreasing.  A window qualifying at $M$ therefore
qualified at $N$, and its empty set at $M$ is a subset of its empty set at
$N$.
\end{proof}

The frontier guard must also cover dismissed stones that become legal only
through earlier dismissed stones.

\begin{lemma}[Frontier chain closure]
\label{lem:L9}
\upshape
In the coupling of \cref{thm:T3}, let $N(x)$ denote the node at which an
$X$-stone $x$ is introduced.  Call $x$ \emph{frontier-clear} if
\[
  \dist(x,y)>8D(N(x))
  \quad\textup{for every }y\in
  \Prot(N(x))\setminus
  (\Legal(P(N(x)))\cup\Stones(P(N(x)))),
\]
where $D(M)=D_M$ and $P(M)=P_M$ for a node $M$.
Then:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]\upshape
  \item if every ghost-legal dismissed stone is frontier-clear, as enforced
    by \cref{zone:Z5}, then every ghost-illegal dismissed stone is
    frontier-clear; and
  \item for every AND node $N$ visited by the coupling, no ghost-empty cell of
    $\Prot(N)\setminus(\Legal(P_N)\cup\Stones(P_N))$ ever becomes legal in
    the real game while remaining illegal in the ghost.
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Any ghost-empty cell that is real-legal but ghost-illegal has an $X$-stone as
a legality witness.  Shared root stones, attacker stones, and searched
placements occur in both positions.  Cells of $Y$ are ghost-occupied and do
not occur in the applications of this lemma.  Inductively, the real legality
of an $X$-stone traces through a chain
$x_0,x_1,\ldots,x_m$ of $X$-stones, where $x_0$ is legal through a shared
stone and consecutive links have distance at most $8$.

For (a), suppose a ghost-illegal $x_j$, with $j\ge1$, is not frontier-clear.
There is a protected but not-yet-legal cell $y$ at $N(x_j)$ with
$\dist(x_j,y)\le8D(N(x_j))$.  The prefix of the chain gives
$\dist(x_0,x_j)\le8j$.  The stones $x_1,\ldots,x_j$ are later defender
placements, so
\[
  j\le D(N(x_0))-1,
  \qquad
  D(N(x_j))\le D(N(x_0))-j.
\]
By \cref{prop:prot-mono} and monotonic growth of ghost legality, $y$ was also
protected but not legal at $N(x_0)$.  Therefore
\[
  \dist(x_0,y)
  \le8j+8(D(N(x_0))-j)
  =8D(N(x_0)),
\]
contradicting the frontier-clear property of the ghost-legal stone $x_0$.

For (b), suppose such a protected cell $y$ becomes real-legal while
ghost-illegal.  It is within distance $8$ of the last chain stone $x_m$.
Every defender placement before $T$ has $D(N(x_m))\ge1$, so
\[
  \dist(x_m,y)\le8\le8D(N(x_m)),
\]
contradicting part (a).
\end{proof}

The repository proof document states part~(b) without the ghost-empty
restriction; only the restricted form above is used, in step~A3.

The proof will compare the actual play with a certificate walk using
canonical difference sets rather than an order relation between positions.

\begin{definition}[Real--ghost coupling]
\label{def:D12}
The proof of \cref{thm:T3} couples a real position $R$ to a ghost position
$G$ on $\mathcal C$.  The positions have the same ply index, mover, and
budget, and have identical attacker stones.  Define the canonical, disjoint
defender-stone differences.  For a position
$Q=(\sigma_Q,m_Q,b_Q)$, write
\[
\begin{aligned}
  \Stones_\D(Q)&:=\{c\in\operatorname{dom}\sigma_Q:\sigma_Q(c)=\D\},\\
  X&=\Stones_\D(R)\setminus\Stones_\D(G),&
  Y&=\Stones_\D(G)\setminus\Stones_\D(R).
\end{aligned}
\]
The coupling maintains these invariants at every visited node $N$:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]\upshape
  \item every $x\in X$ entered at a dismissal step whose node certified
    $x\notin\Prot$;
  \item every $y\in Y$ entered as a ghost filler, which is a searched
    placement of its node;
  \item $X\cap\Prot(N)=\varnothing$, by part (i) and
    \cref{prop:prot-mono}; and
  \item every $x\in X$ is frontier-clear.  Condition \cref{zone:Z5} enforces
    this for ghost-legal dismissals.  The chain closure in \cref{lem:L9}
    passes it to ghost-illegal dismissals.
\end{enumerate}
A dismissal-history set $\widehat X\supseteq X$ records every cell ever added
to $X$ and never removes a cell.  It is used when the completion argument
must remember an earlier dismissal after the canonical difference has
changed.
\end{definition}
