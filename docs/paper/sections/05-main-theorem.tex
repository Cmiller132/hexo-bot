\section{Main theorem: dismissal soundness}
\label{sec:main-theorem}

The certificate controls a ghost play, not the defender's actual choices.
The proof compiles its designated OR moves into a real attacker strategy and
maps every real defender placement to one certificate AND edge.  Direct
obligations preserve attacker moves, ranked seed guards preserve future
occupancy, and the completion guards prevent a defender win during the
mapping.

\begin{theorem}[Local-clock, pathwise dismissal soundness]
\label{thm:T3}
\upshape
Let $\mathcal C$ obey \cref{def:D9,def:D10,def:D11,def:D12,def:D14,def:D15,def:D16}
and be zone-carrying under \cref{zone:Z2,zone:Z4} and
\hyperref[zone:Z5prime]{clause \textup{(Z5$'$)}}.  For every real defender
play against the compiled attacker strategy, either the real attacker wins
strictly earlier than the mapped ghost resolution, or the play maps to a
finite certificate path and $\A$ completes by that path's declared
resolution.  Consequently $\A$ wins by the global maximum $T$, and every
certified dismissal is sound.
\end{theorem}

\begin{plainterms}
The real defender may play any legal cell, including one omitted from the
certificate.  The ghost consumes a searched reply on the same ply and keeps
following the finite certificate.  The theorem says that the resulting board
differences cannot remove a required attacker move or create a defender win
before the recorded attacker resolution.
\end{plainterms}

\begin{proof}
Couple the real position $R$, initially $P_0$, to a ghost walk $G$ on
$\mathcal C$.  Maintain equal ply, mover, and budget, identical attacker
stones, and the canonical sets $X$, $Y$, and $\widehat X$ from
\cref{def:D12}.  Process a typed leaf immediately when the ghost walk reaches
it.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Step O: an OR node with designated placement $c$.}
The live placement role puts $c$ in $\Prot$ at every current ancestor.  The
invariant $X\cap\Prot=\varnothing$ gives $c\notin X$.  The ghost position
leaves $c$ empty, so $c\notin Y$: a cell in $Y$ is ghost-occupied.  It follows
that $c$ is empty in the real position.  Clause \cref{zone:Z4} supplies an
attacker stone or unaffected root stone that is a legality witness in both
positions.  The compiled strategy therefore places $c$ in both games.  Their
attacker-stone sets remain identical.

Suppose the OR step is an OR-COMPLETION leaf.  Immediately before $c$, the
other five cells of its named window are shared attacker stones.  The window
is attacker-complete in the ghost after $c$, so it had no ghost defender
stone before the move and contains no $Y$-cell.  The cell $c$ is not in $X$.
The same placement therefore completes the same real window at the declared
ply.  If the real placement completes another window that is not complete in
the ghost, the extension and deletion transfers of
\cref{lem:L5,lem:L6} make it an immediate, strictly earlier real attacker win.
In either event the theorem's conclusion holds.  At an ordinary OR node,
discharge the placement role after its deadline check and descend to the
exact child.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Step A: an AND node $N$ and a real defender placement $d$.}
Exactly one of three cases applies.  The cell $d$ is ghost-occupied, it is
ghost-empty and searched, or it is ghost-empty and unsearched.  Before the
cases, fix the following operation for any step on which the ghost cannot
copy $d$.

\smallskip\noindent
\emph{Filler subroutine.}
Choose any $d_0\in S(N)$.  Such a cell exists independently of the current
real reply by \cref{def:D9,def:D11}.  It is empty and legal in the ghost
position.  If $d_0\in X$, placing it in the ghost removes $d_0$ from $X$.
Otherwise the placement adds $d_0$ to $Y$.  Recompute the canonical
differences after the move.  Every new $Y$-cell is a searched ghost filler,
and this operation can only shrink $X$.

\smallskip\noindent
\textbf{Case A1: $d$ is ghost-empty and searched.}
Both games place at $d$ and the ghost descends to its exact certificate child.
The two placements add the same defender stone, so $X$ and $Y$ do not change.
Ply, mover, and budget advance identically under \cref{def:D4}.

\smallskip\noindent
\textbf{Case A2: $d$ is ghost-occupied.}
The attacker stones are shared and $d$ is empty in the real position.  Thus
$d\in Y$.  The real placement cancels this difference and removes $d$ from
$Y$.  Run the filler subroutine in the ghost and descend to the exact child
of the selected filler.  The two games again consume one defender placement
and retain the same mover and budget.

\smallskip\noindent
\textbf{Case A3: $d$ is ghost-empty and unsearched.}
First suppose $d$ is ghost-legal.  If it were protected, then
$d\in\Zdir(N)$, and \cref{zone:Z2} would put it in $S(N)$.  Hence a
ghost-legal dismissal is outside the current protected set.  It is also
outside $\Zseed(N)$, so it satisfies the seed hypothesis of
\cref{lem:L9prime} for every protected cell that is not yet ghost-legal.

Now suppose $d$ is ghost-illegal.  Its real legality must come from a chain of
real-only defender stones.  The first protected-occupation lemma says that
placing it cannot be the first real-only occupation of a currently protected
cell.  It is therefore outside the current protected set in this case as
well.

The real game places at $d$.  Add $d$ to $X$ and to the monotone history
$\widehat X$.  Run the filler subroutine and descend to its child.  The ranked
seed argument and reachable-obligation nesting preserve
$X\cap\Prot=\varnothing$.  Occupancy is accounted for by the canonical
update, and the radius-$8$ chain accounts for the legality frontier.  These
are channels C1 and C3 of \cref{lem:L3}.  The fixed-window conclusion of
\cref{lem:L12} accounts for channel C2: no real defender window completes
before the mapped attacker wins or first enters that window.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{A WIN leaf.}
Let $W$ be a named attacker-alive witness.  Every ghost attacker stone in
$W$ is shared.  Each other cell of $W$ belongs to $E(W,P_L)$ and carries a
live witness-empty role, so it is not in $X$ at leaf entry.  Since $W$ is
attacker-alive in the ghost, it contains no ghost defender stone and hence no
$Y$-cell.  The complete real and ghost masks of $W$, including their empty
sets, agree at leaf entry.

The witness has one empty when its count is $5$, or two empties when its count
is $4$ and the attacker budget is $2$.  By \cref{lem:L1}, these empties lie
within distance $2$ of permanent shared attacker stones.  They are legal in
the real position, remain empty, and can be filled without an intervening
defender placement.  The real attacker completes $W$ within the current turn
and by the leaf's declared resolution.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{A LOSS leaf.}
Apply the same compressed-obligation argument to every $W\in\mathcal T$.
All attacker stones are shared, every ghost empty of $W$ is protected through
leaf entry and is therefore $X$-free, and attacker-aliveness excludes a
$Y$-cell.  Thus the real and ghost masks and empty sets of every named witness
agree at leaf entry.  The real empty-set family has the checked transversal
number
\[
  \tau(\mathcal T)>b.
\]

The real defender cannot satisfy $\ownwinnow$ at this leaf.  Consider a
putative defender witness window.  If it has no ever-dismissed fill, the mask
inequality \eqref{eq:MI}, the shared attacker mask, and the equal budget
transfer the same immediate-win predicate to the ghost, contrary to the
mandatory leaf check.  If it has an ever-dismissed fill, the first-dismissal
and exposure argument in \cref{lem:L12} includes all $b$ placements remaining
at the LOSS leaf and precludes completion during the current defender turn.

After the leaf-entry checks, discharge the witness-empty roles.  Let the real
defender choose any complete nonterminal remainder $H$ of its turn.  Since
$|H|\le b<\tau(\mathcal T)$, this set of placements cannot hit every named
empty set.  There is some $W_H\in\mathcal T$ with
\[
  E(W_H,P_L)\cap H=\varnothing.
\]
The window remains alive for $\A$.  It has at most two surviving empties, and
\cref{lem:L1} places them within distance $2$ of permanent shared attacker
stones.  The attacker fills them during the following turn and completes
$W_H$ by leaf-ply${}+b+2$.  No obligation protection is needed after leaf
entry; the universal choice of $W_H$ supplies a witness that survived the
actual remainder.

Every nonterminal operation descends one edge of the finite certificate.
Thus, unless the real attacker has already won strictly earlier, the coupled
play reaches an OR-COMPLETION, WIN, or LOSS transfer and resolves by that
path's declared ply.  The global $T$ is the maximum of these declared
resolutions.  The compiled strategy wins against every legal real defender
play by $T$.
\end{proof}
