\section{Open problems}
\label{sec:open-problems}

\paragraph{Sharpened budget.}
The completion guard in \cref{thm:T4} counts every defender placement before
the horizon.  A sharper budget would count quiet placements $F$ together with
a per-window forced-hit capacity $H_W$.  Proving this bound requires
per-branch worst-case bookkeeping over the certificate DAG.  The soundness
result in \cref{thm:T3} does not require the sharpening, at the cost of wider
zones for deep loose certificates.

\paragraph{Sharper frontier bands.}
The frontier guard \cref{zone:Z5} uses the worst-case radius $8D_N$ needed by
the chain argument in \cref{lem:L9}.  Each link also costs defender tempo, and
the completion guard already limits that tempo.  A joint accounting of time
and distance should shrink the band substantially; that accounting is
unwritten.

\paragraph{Pairing at the threshold.}
The density bound in \cref{cor:T8.1} leaves the case $k=7$ and $g=3$ open,
where the required matched density is exactly one.  This threshold case does
not affect Hexo, whose winning length is $k=6$, and
\cref{thm:T8} already rules out the required Hexo pairing.  Existence at the
threshold remains unsettled.

\paragraph{Formalization.}
The model and results through \cref{thm:T8} are finite combinatorics and are
feasible to formalize in Lean or Coq.  Such a formalization would yield a
verified reference checker
for \cref{zone:Z1,zone:Z2,zone:Z4,zone:Z5}.
