\begin{abstract}
Threat-space search in Hexo has large defender branching because a legal
reply may be far from the current tactics.  We give finite certificates that
restrict those replies without changing a verified attacker win.  The main
result is a horizon-parameterized dismissal theorem with ranked obligation
guards and separate completion guards.  Exact-copy forcing gates permit a
target-specific debit, while sparse LOSS witnesses, a sharp pairing threshold,
and potential-function bounds describe complementary parts of the search.
The paper states the verifier contracts, records their proved limits, and
separates machine-checked finite claims from mathematical proofs.
\end{abstract}
