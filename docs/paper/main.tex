\begin{abstract}
Threat-space search in Hexo has large defender branching because every legal
reply must ordinarily be considered.  We give a certified restriction based
on local defender budgets, cell-ranked obligation guards, and separate
completion guards for touched and virgin windows.  A verifier accepting these
conditions preserves an attacker win over the full legal move set and obtains
the resolution bound of the selected certificate path.  Further results give
an extendable-hit kernel for forced regions, sparse LOSS witnesses,
branch-indexed substitution, and finite certificate DAGs.  Companion results
cover global pairing impossibility, potential-function blocking certificates,
local domination patterns, and mechanized evidence for the predecessor search
heuristic.
\end{abstract}
